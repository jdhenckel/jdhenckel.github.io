animikiig the thunderers

anishinaabe people's aadizookanag stories Ojibwa Texts 1919 #18 p.190-195

dibaajimod told by gaagige- forever binesi bird (thunderbird)



mewinzha long ago anishinaabeg the people omaa here gaawiin no wiikaa ever gii-zhaasiiwag they did not go i'imaa there (to) animikii- thunderbird neyaashiing cape, point, peninsula, sound. moozhag often imaa there animikiig thunderers gii-noodaagoziwag they made a sound, pane always aanakwad cloud gii-ayaamagad (it is) that it was there ogidaajiw on top of the mountain; mii so dash then gaa-onji-gotamowaad (it is) because of this that they had feared it. o'omaa here inakakeyaa towards the direction of animikii-wikwedong thunder bay gii-izhaawag they went anishinaabeg (the) people. owidi over there gaye and inakake in such direction zhaawanong to the south minong isle royale gii-izhi-giiwiitaaya'wag they in such a way were all around.


ningoding at one time idash then oshkinaweg (the) new relatives, youths gii-ikidoowag they said: "Daga let it be that makadekedaa let us fast wii-waabandandaa most likely let's see it aaniin how ezhiwebadogwe in such a way sound is happening i'imaa there noondaagwak that they make a sound!" mii so dash then gii-makadekewaad (it is) that they fast, animikii-wikwedong thunder bay gii-onji-maajiitaawag they started from. gaawiikaa never gii-wiisinisiiwag they did not eat biinish until ginwezh a long time. nishwaasagonagak as it was eight days idash then gii-aamajiwewag they walk on the hill imaa there waajiwing on the mountain. eshkam little by little apiji very enigok with much effort noondaagoziwan they, the others make a sound animikiin the other thunderbirds. ningoding at one time idash then dibishkoo similar to gegoo something baakaakonigaadeg (it is) that it is opening mii so iw that ge-ini'kaanig next that it behaves aanakwad cloud. mii so dash then imaa there gii-waabamaawaad (it is) that they saw the other(s) niizh two gichi-binesiwag great birds, niizh two gaye also ayaa'aansag little ones that are; mii so go well ishkode fire bapasangaabiwaad as they blink again and again odizhinaawaawaan as they in such a way perceive the other(s). ganabaj maybe niizhing twice ekoonamang breath(s) mii so iw that minik many gaa-waabamaawaad (it is) that they had seen the other(s); miinawaa again gii-gibise it closed aanakwad (the) cloud.


mii so dash then gaa-kidod it is that s/he had said bezhig one oshkinawe new relative, youth: "aaniish how then mii so sa ! gii-waabamang it is that we have seen the other(s)," odinanaan s/he said to the other(s) owiijiiwaganan her/his friend. mii so dash then ekidod it is that s/he says a'aw that one in motion bezhig one: "geyaabi still daga let it be that wii-waabamaadaa most likely let's see it!" ginwezh a long time gaganoonidiwag they speak with one another. “mii so debiseg it is that it satisfies me,” gii-ikido s/he said a’aw that one in motion bezhig one. bezhig one idash then geyaabi still wii-izhaa wishes to go imaa there; odaanon gii-anoo-ngiiandomaan s/he in vain tried to convince the other owiijiiwaaganan his/her other friend. mii so dash then ani-maajaad that away s/he leaves gii’iwed as that s/he returns home, mii so iw that waasigan lightning gii-bi-izhisenig that it in such a way s/he is approaching flying i’imaa there niibawiban s/he had stood a’aw that shkinawe new relative, youth, mii so iw that gii-nisigod it is that s/he was killed.




mii so dash then i’iw that apii moment gii-maajaawaad it is that they left ingiw those animikiig thunderbirds; ishkwaaji lastly idash then gii-waabamaa s/he sees her/him namadabid as s/he is seated gichi-binesi great bird i’imaa there gaa-manitigweyaag that the crooked river flows wajiw mountain ayaamagak that it is there. mii so dash then i’iw that gaa-izhinikaadamowaad it is that they had called it anishinaabeg people animikii thunderbird wajiw mountain, mii so iw that Makes McKay wajiw mountain edamowaad that they call it weyaapiskiwejig those that are.


Mii so dash then i’iw that bijiinag soon, just after gii-izhaawaad it is that they went imaa there neyaashing to the point, gaawiin no geyaabi still ogii-gotasiinaawa they were not afraid of it. aanawi anyway geyaabi still awiya someone ogii-waabamaawan they saw the other(s) i’imaa there neyaashing on the sound, anishinaabeng like people izhinaagoziwan the other(s) appeared as.  asin stone ojiimaaniwa their canoe; apii moment idash then waabamigoowad as they are seen by the people, mii so iw that ezhi-maajii-gwajiwenid it is that the other(s) in such a way began paddling; gwayak straight igo well giizhkaabikaang in the cliff izhi-bindige s/he in such a way enters gwazhiwewan they fear being seen. aanawi anyway ogii-wiikwaji'aawaan they try to do it besho near wii-ganoonaawaad it is that they wish to speak to the other(s).


ningoding once bezhig one anishinaabe person ogii-waabamaan s/he saw the other(s) mishawagaam far in the water ayaanid it is that the other(s) is/are. mii so idash then bii-gichi-animikiikaanig as arrives a great thunderstorm, apiji very bii-gichi-izhiwebadinig as it arrives in such a way happening. mii so dash then gii-noondawaad it is that they hear the other(s) madwe-nagamonid as the other(s) is/are heard from a distance singing.


“bayakwaana’kwak a clear sky maandan this one not in motion ningiizhigom my sky, apiji very much ningotan I fear it.” (3x)


mii so dash then baabige right away gaa-izhi-gichi-anaawedinig as it had in such a way greatly become calm, gaye and dash then baabige right away gaawiin no aanakwad cloud gii-ayaasiinoon it was not there; baabige right away gii-mino-giizhigad it is that it was a good day. mii so iw that gii-bimi-giiwewaad it is that they along go home ingiw those ones in motion memegwesiwag the little people.


moozhag often ogii-waabamaawaan they saw the other(s) iniw the/those other(s) memegwesiwan the little people others, naanigodinong once in awhile idash then ogii-ganoonigowaan they were spoken to by the other(s). “apiji very ozaagi’aan they like it semaan other tobacco,” gii-ikidowag they said anishinaabeg the people. ningoding once in awhile idash then miinawaa again waabamaawaad it is that they saw the other(s) mishawagaam far out in the water egomonid in a canoe* ogii-gagwe-aasikawaawaan they tried to prevent the other(s) i’imaa there ishkwaandemiwang at a doorway asiniing in the rock; geget surely ogii-aasikawaawaan they did prevent the other(s); mii so dash then gaa-izhi-ani-migobagizoowaad as they had in such a waylowered their heads to their chests and hid themselves; gaawiin no biinaabisiiwan the other(s) would not look here. bezhig one idash then ogii-ganoonaan s/he speaks to the other(s) : “aaniin how endiyeg is it that you all live?” odiinaan s/he says to the other(s). bezhig one idash then a’aw that one in motion memegwesi little person gii-kido said, oganoon s/he speaks to the other(s) wajiwaad to her/his friend *; “daga let it be that, giin you inaabin perceive ! eniwek very much giin you anishinaabeng like a human being gidizhinaagoz you are handsome.”



mii so dash then gii-anishkaad that s/he rose up bezhig one a’aw that one in motion memegwesi little person, mishiingwe s/he has hair on her/his face. mii so dash then gii-bagidinaawaad it is that they released the other(s) maano let it be that zhi-bimishkaanid that in such a way the other(s) go along in the canoe. mii so dash then gwayak straight igo well giizhkaabikaang to the steep cliif; gii-baagise it flew open bangi a little bit i’iw that one not in motion aazhibik across the stone, gaa-izhi-bindigewaad as in such a way they had entered ojiimaaniwaa their canoe gaye also; madwe-bapiwag they are heard from a distance laughing, abwiin paddles gaye also madweyakwisinoon they are heard from a distance.
