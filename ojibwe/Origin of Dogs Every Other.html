wenjiiwaad that they come from animoshag dogs

15. The Origin of Dogs (p. 170-175)


onji- from anishinaabe (the) people's aadizookaanag stories

Ojibwa Texts II (printed 1919)


animikii thunder wikwedong bay

Ft. William


dibaajimod as told by gaagige- forever binesii great bird/thunderbird

Told by John Binesi sometime between 1903 and 1905



ingoding at one time niizh two anishinaabeg people bimishkaawaad it is that they paddle jiimaaning in a canoe gichigamiing on the sea iwidi over there giiwedinong to the north inagage after awhile mii so iw that gaa-izhi-gichi-noodininig as it had in such a way been very windy mii so idash then gaa-izhi-webaashiwaad it was that they had been in such a way been blown away mishawagaam far into the middle of the water gichigamiing on the sea gaawiin no ogii-waabandansiinaawa they did not see it aki land. apiji very gii-gichi-noodinini it was very windy gaawiin no gii-gwanabishkaasiiwag they did not flip over. ginwezh for a long while gichigamiing on the sea gii-babaa-ayaawag from place to place they went. ningoding at one time gii-agwaaweba'oowag they were carried agaamakiing to the other side of the land. gii-gabaawaad as they stood owaabandaanaawaa they see it awiya someone bimi-kawenid another that makes a track gichanishinaaben another great human being. mii so dash then gii-zegisiwaad as they were in fear ogii-gopiwidoonaawa they carried it ojiimaaniwaa their canoe mii so dash then i'imaa there anamoonag underneath gii-gaazowaad it was that they hid. ningoding once onondaanaawaa they hear it gegoo something madwesininig as s/he makes a noise from hitting something; mii so dash then inaabiwaad as they look to a certain place waabandaanaawaa they see it gichi-bikwak a great arrow atenig as it is there. mii so dash then geget surely zegisiwaad it is that they are frightened. wiiba soon go well ogii-bi-odizigoowaan another approached them gichi-anishinaaben another great human, oganoonigoowaan they are spoken to by another: nishiime my younger sibling, gego don’t zegisikegon be afraid! niin I misaabe giant egooyaan it is that I am called. gaawiin no niin I nimbanaaji'aasiig I do not destroy them anishinaabeg human beings odigoowaan s/he is spoken to by another. owaabamaawaan they see another adikwan another caribou zhegwazonid as another hangs from a belt, mii so wiin s/he iniw another bebaabimwaad as they wander wii-amwaad as s/he wants to eat another a'aw that one in motion misaabe giant. mii so idash then gaa-izhi-odaapinigowaad it is that they had in such a way been taken apiji very mindidoowan the other is big iniw the other(s) misaaben another giant. ogii-bindoomonaan s/he took another(s) inside the chest (of his garment) iniw that other(s) anishinaaben other person(s) a'aw that misaabe giant. mii so dash then gii-giiwewinigowaad it is that they must have gone home endaanid that the other resides. apii moment, time dash then, but, and gaa-bindigewaad as they had entered, naa you know naage after awhile gii-bindige s/he entered aw that one in motion wiindigoo ice giant. mii so dash then enaad it is that s/he says to the other iniw the other misaaben giant a'aw that wiindigoo ice giant: "anishinaabeg people gidayaawag you have them," odinaan s/he says to the other. ogii-gaanigowaan the other(s) were hidden biindig inside endaanid that resides the other iniw the other misaaben the other giant jibwaa-bindigenid as before the other enters iniw the other wiindigoon (the) other ice giant. "gaawiin no anishinaabe (a) human being nindaawaasiig I have not," ikido s/he says a'aw, awe, aw that one in motion wiindigoo ice giant. "geget surely," ikido says a'aw that wiindigoo ice giant. mii so igo, go, gwa, igwa well, um, ah ezhi-noondaagozid that/as in such a way s/he is heard ikwanaamod that s/he inhales. "oop!" inhallation sound ikwanaamod as s/he inhales a'aw that one in motion wiindigoo ice giant, epiichi-misawenimaad as to a certain extent s/he desires the other anishinaaben the other human being wii-amwaad that s/he wishes to eat the other. gegapii finally igo well nishkaadizi s/he is angry ginwezh for a long while gaganoonidiwaad as they speak to one another. gegapii finally gaye and, also wiin s/he misaabe (the) giant nishkaadizi is angry. mitigoonaagan (a) wooden dish ateni is placed/situated imaa there biindig inside endaad (where it is) that that s/he resides. mii so dash then i'iw that gaa-baakikwaadang as s/he had pried it up, mii so dash then imaa there animoons little dog eyaad that s/he is i'imaa there anaamaya’ii'onaaganing under the dish, odayensan has the other a'aw that one in motion misaabe giant. "daga please, bashkwadaash hairless awi-miigaazh go on (and) fight a'aw that maji-anishinaabe bad person.


mii so iw that one not in motion, the other(s) geget surely bazigwiid that/as s/he rises a’aw that one that is in motion animoons little dog; gii-bapawi s/he shook, mii so dash then gii-ani-mindidod that/as s/he away became big. eshkam more and more gii-ani-bapawi s/he away shook a’aw that one in motion animosh dog, apiji very gii-ani-mindido s/he away became big a’aw that one in motion animosh dog. apii (at this) moment idash then wayaabamaad (it is) that s/he sees the other animoon the other dog mindidomid that the other is big gii-ani-zaaga’am s/he went out a’aw that wiindigoo ice giant. ogii-gagaanzomaan s/he goads the other odayan her/his dog zhi-nisaad that in a certain way, in such a way s/he kill the other wiindigoon the ice giant other. mii so idash then gii-miigaanaad that s/he fought the other iniw that other wiindigoon (the) ice giant other a'aw that animosh dog biinish until ogii-nisaan s/he killed the other. apii (at this) moment idash then gaa-nisaad s/he had killed the other, miinawaa, miinwaa, miina again gii-bindige s/he entered a'aw that animosh dog. gii-bapawi s/he shook her/himself miinawaa again gii-bindige s/he entered imaa there anaamaya'ii underneath onaaganishing in the castoff bowl.


mii so idash then egowaad it is that they were told by the other(s) iniw that other(s) misaaben the giant other: "nishime'idog my respected little sisters and brothers, onjida on purpose ningii-inendam I thought zhi-bi-zhaayeg that in such a way you all come this way omaa here. wii-waabaminagog it is that I wished to see you all. mii so sa ! indawaa therefore zhi-giiweyeg that in such a way you all return home. wa'awe that one in motion nindayens my little dog gimiininim I give to you all. gaawiin no miinawaa again da-bi-giiwesii s/he will not come this way home. gaawiin no awiya someone animosh dog ayaasii is not iwidi over there giinawaa you all endanakiiyeg that you all live. maagizhaa maybe eniwek very much ga-daabaji'aawaa you will use her/him, ge and gegaa almost anishinaabeng as a human being da-izhiwebizi s/he will in such a way act.


mii so idash then gaa-izhi-ganoonaad that s/he had in such a way spoken to the other iniw that other(s) odayensan her/his dog a'aw that one in motion misaabe giant: "daga let it be that giiwewizh take home ogow these ones in motion nishiimeyag my little sisters and brothers!" ogii-naansibiiwinaan s/he carried the other to the water iniw that, those other(s) odayensan his/her dog; nawaj more gii-ani-mindido s/he went on away growing bashkwadash hairless. ogii-niibawi'aan s/he placed the other standing iniw that other odayan her/his dog, mii so dash then imaa there ogidaawigan on her/his back gii-asaad as/that s/he placed the other(s) iniw those others anishinaaben her/his people. "haaw alright mii so iw that izhi-maajzhiizh in such a way start carry him/her, gwayak straight endana'kiiwaad that they live izhiwizh in such a way carry him/her!"


mii so dash then gii-maajiibatood as, that s/he started running over to there a'aw that one in motion bashkwadaash hairless, eshkam more and more gii-ani-mindido s/he on away became big; gii-aazhawgamibatood as, that across the water s/he ran over to there iwe that gichi-gichigamiing ocean; ayaabitawokaad at half her/his legs eta just gii-akobii was the height of the water, biinish until gii-dagoshing as, that s/he arrived omaa here aking on land.


*eshkam more and more miinawaa again gii-bi-agaachiinyin s/he was towards becoming small apii (at this) moment gegaa almost ba-oditang that s/he reached it owe this aki land, biinish until miinawaa again animoonsing like a little dog gii-inigini s/he was the size of a'aw that one in motion bashkwadaash hairless. mii so idash then gaa-izhi-naganigoowaad as in such a way the other had left them behind iniw those others animoon dog others, wiinawaa they dash then gii-giiwewag the went home endaawaad that they reside. banimaa later dash then ningoding once ogii-odizigowaan to where they were the other came animoon dog other, mii so go well iw that gii-bi-inawemigowaad that approaching, the other was related to them. mii so go well iw that miziwe everywhere gaa-izhiwebak that is what had happened aking in, at, on, by the land gii-odizigoowaad when the other came to them animoshan dog other ingiw those anishinaabeg people. mii so iw that bijiinag soon apii moment gii-ayaawaad as, that they were animoog dogs o'omaa here aking on earth; mii so aw that bashkwadash hairless oniijaanisa her/his child, niibowa many daswewaanagizi are so many types of a'aw that one in motion animosh dog ezhinikaazod as, that s/he is called. mii so go well aw that bashkwadash hairless gakina all imaa there wenjiiwaad it is that is comes from her/him ingiw those animoshag dogs miziwe everywhere noongom/nongwa now eyaawaad that they are animoog dogs.


mii so sa ! ekosid that is the extent it is
